The SolarChem Ontology: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 depicts the SolarChem ontology and its three associated modules: SolarChem Photocatalysis, SolarChem Electrocatalysis and SolarChem Photoelectrocatalysis. The three modules import this core ontology to extend it with specific terms about the type of catalysis experiment regarding input product role, conditions, conversion metrics, etc.
The ontology revolves about the concept `solar:ExperimentExecution`, that refers to the execution of a catalysis experiment and its characteristics reported from a scientific article. The articles where the experiments information is extracted are described with `bibo:Article`, which in turn include its authors (`schema:Person`), their affiliation country (`schema:Country`) and the journal where it is published (`bibo:Journal`). Experiments are performed under specific conditions (`solar:Condition`), such as temperature (`solar:TemperatureCondition`), pressure (`solar:PressureCondition`), pH (`solar:pHCondition`), reaction time (`solar:ReactionTimeCondition`), reaction medium (`solar:ReactionMediumCondition`, which can be either liquid or gas), operation mode (`solar:OperationModeCondition`, which can be either batch or continuous), and electrochemical configuration (`solar:ElectrochemicalConfiguration`). Additionally, for photocatalysis and photoelectrocatalysis experiments, the type of light source (`solar:LightSource`) is also described, as it plays an essential role in the experiment execution. Light sources may be of a particular type (e.g. ultraviolet, sun light, etc), be produced by a lamp (`solar:Lamp`), have a certain wavelength (`solar:Wavelength`), irradiance (`solar:Irradiance`), power (`solar:Power`) and illuminated area (`solar:IlluminatedArea`). Experiments use chemical entities (`chebi:24431`) as input (`solar:Input`), and produce others as output products (`solar:Output`). The input substances play a certain role (`solar:MaterialTransformationRole`) in experiments (e.g. catalyst, reagent, co-catalyst), and are displayed with a specific band gap (`solar:BandGap`) and surface area (`solar:SurfaceArea`) of reaction. The output substances produced are measured with their quantity, and described further with conversion metrics (`solar:ConversionMetric`) and electrochemical metrics (`solar:ElectrochemicalMetric`).
